Welcome to Statistics for Cybersecurity

Academic Year 2025/2026 – A foundational course exploring how statistical reasoning, probabilistic modeling, and data analysis can be applied to detect, prevent, and understand cyber threats.

Course Overview

The Statistics for Cybersecurity course provides students with a deep understanding of how statistical techniques can be leveraged in network defense and cyber threat detection. Through practical assignments, you will analyze datasets, model uncertainty, and evaluate anomalies that indicate malicious behavior.

Throughout this course, students will learn how to use descriptive and inferential statistics, probability distributions, and correlation measures to improve cybersecurity posture and data-driven decision-making.

Core Topics

  • Understanding and building datasets for cybersecurity analysis
  • Exploring probability distributions in network events
  • Detecting anomalies with statistical intrusion detection systems
  • Correlation and regression applied to threat intelligence data

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will be able to:

  • Design and interpret statistical models to detect abnormal network behavior.
  • Use distributions and hypothesis testing to identify cyber anomalies.
  • Apply data visualization and probabilistic reasoning in threat analysis.
  • Understand how NGFWs, IDS, and IPS leverage statistics to enhance protection.
